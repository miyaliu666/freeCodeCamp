# --description--

The verb `recognize` means to identify someone or something seen before or to acknowledge the existence or legality of something. It's used when someone is familiar with a person or thing and can identify them based on previous knowledge or experience.

# --question--

## --text--

What does `recognize` generally mean in a context like `She quickly recognized her friend in the crowd`?

## --answers--

She introduced her friend to others.

### --feedback--

`Introducing someone` is different from `recognizing` them, which means identifying someone already known.

---

She forgot who her friend was.

### --feedback--

To `recognize` means to remember or identify, not to forget.

---

She identified her friend who she had seen before.

---

She was meeting her friend for the first time.

### --feedback--

`Recognizing` implies previous knowledge, so it's not about a first-time meeting.
